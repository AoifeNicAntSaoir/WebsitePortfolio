Week 9 - March 13 - 16

Image Comparison


I spent this week researching & trying different methods of comparing images in Python. I came up with the following methods:

  • Mean Squared Error Equation
  • Structural Similarity Index Equation
  • OpenCV library contours
  • PIL ImageChops library difference()

To normalise the images, I ensured they were at the same size 8x8px and in grayscale format

Mean Squared Error Equation




Using the sklearn.metrics mean_squared_error(img1, img2) method to measure the quality of an estimatory - it is always non-negative and the closer to 0 the better

MSE

Structural Similarity Index Equation




Using the skimage.measure compare_ssim(img1, img2) to compare the images. SSIM is used for measuring the similarity between two images. The SSIM index is a full reference metric; in other words, the measurement or prediction of image quality is based on an initial uncompressed or distortion-free image as reference.

SSIM
